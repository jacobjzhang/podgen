# Customer Segments

Applying Charlie Munger's advice: find markets with weak competition.

## Why Audio?

Non-technical users who:
- Can't read while doing their primary activity
- Have dedicated listening time
- Want to stay informed but are underserved by current tech

---

## High Potential Segments

### 1. Commuters & Drivers
**Who:** Truckers, delivery drivers, rideshare drivers, sales reps, long commuters

- 2+ hours/day of dedicated listening time
- Can't read, need audio
- Want news but hate radio ads
- Underserved by podcasts (too long, wrong topics)

**Positioning:** "Your morning drive briefing"

---

### 2. Financial Advisor → Client Communication (B2B)
**Who:** Financial advisors sending updates to clients; clients are typically older, wealthy

- Advisors currently send PDF reports no one reads
- Clients prefer audio over reading dense reports
- High willingness to pay (B2B)
- Trust relationship already exists

**Positioning:** "Send your clients a personalized market podcast"

**Why competition is weak:**
- Existing tools are text-based newsletters
- No one is doing personalized audio for wealth management
- Compliance-friendly (can review before sending)

---

### 3. Small Business Owners
**Who:** Restaurant owners, contractors, shop owners, franchise operators

- No time to sit and read industry news
- Need to know about regulations, local economy, trends
- Listen while opening shop, doing inventory, driving to jobs
- Not at desks, not on computers

**Positioning:** "Your industry briefing while you open shop"

---

### 4. Retirees & Elderly
**Who:** 65+, heavy news consumers, less comfortable with apps/screens

- Vision issues make reading small text difficult
- Comfortable with audio (radio generation)
- Heavy news consumption habits
- Underserved by tech industry (everyone chases young demos)
- Family members could set it up for them

**Positioning:** "News you can listen to, not squint at"

---

### 5. Real Estate Agents
**Who:** Residential and commercial agents, always mobile

- Constantly driving between showings
- Need market updates, local news, interest rate changes
- Time-sensitive information (rates, inventory)
- Would pay for competitive edge
- Already consume podcasts

**Positioning:** "Your market update between showings"

---

### 6. Healthcare Workers
**Who:** Nurses, aides, techs - long shifts, on feet

- Exhausted after shifts, won't read
- Can listen during commute or breaks
- Want to stay informed about world
- Large population, underserved

---

### 7. Non-Desk Workers (Blue Collar)
**Who:** Construction, warehouse, manufacturing, maintenance

- Can listen while working (earbuds common)
- Not sitting at computers
- Want news, sports, topics they care about
- Current options: radio (ads, not personalized) or music

---

## Lowest Competition + Highest Need

1. **Financial advisor → client communication** - No one doing this, high willingness to pay
2. **Local/hyperlocal news** - Newspapers are dead, no replacement
3. **Industry-specific briefings for non-desk workers** - Completely underserved

---

## Distribution Angles

- **B2B:** Sell to advisors, agencies, franchises who distribute to their people
- **Scheduled delivery:** Morning briefing at 6am, ready when you wake up
- **Family setup:** Kids set it up for parents/grandparents
- **Embed in existing workflows:** CRM integrations, email delivery
